# Provincial Travel Guide JSON Dataset

## Purpose and scope

TL;DR: A province-level JSON dataset (11 provinces) structured exactly to your schema, biased toward 2024–2026 official/primary sources where possible, and using clearly reasonable estimates where schedules/fares are volatile.

The dataset is designed for a Thailand Travel Guide app that needs practical in-province transport modes, Bangkok→province and intra-province routes, authentic local foods, real accommodation examples, safety warnings, eco/environment IDs, and climate/terrain metadata.

## Source set

Transport schedules and operators were anchored on operator-owned pages where available (notably intercity bus/airport bus and standard ferry operators), e.g. entity["company","Bell Travel Service","thai intercity transport"] departure times for Suvarnabhumi↔Pattaya, entity["company","Roong Reuang Coach","thai bus operator"] / Airport Pattaya Bus schedules and fares, and Pattaya Bus schedules/fare. citeturn35view0turn32search0turn32search1

Official tourism and province pages were used heavily for “what is this province known for” and for local specialties/food identity, especially through entity["organization","Tourism Authority of Thailand","national tourism organization"] (Amazing Thailand). citeturn20search8turn25search10turn24search7turn47search3turn24search9

For eco/habitat grounding and provincial features (e.g., Rayong mangrove ecosystems at Thung Prong Thong), Amazing Thailand attraction pages were used. citeturn29search0turn29search4

Where a province has a clear air gateway, entity["company","Bangkok Airways","thai airline"] official airport/office pages were used (e.g., Trat Airport and its local airport office number). citeturn40search2turn40search5

For accommodation “bookingUrl”, province landing pages from entity["company","Agoda","online travel agency"] and entity["company","Booking.com","online travel agency"] were used, because they’re stable entry points for province-wide listings. citeturn45search0turn46search0turn46search1turn46search2turn46search3turn12search1turn12search45turn14search26turn14search31turn14search9

Season framing (monsoon vs. dry) is aligned to Thailand’s standard 3-season model (rainy/southwest monsoon mid-May–mid-October, etc.) from an English-language Thai government resource. citeturn30search4

## Notes on schedule and fare volatility

Some route fields (departureTimes, baseFare, frequency) are inherently volatile in Thailand (operator changes, holiday surcharges, weather-related cancellations, pier changes). For those routes, the dataset intentionally uses:
- Representative “anchor” departure times (e.g., morning / midday / evening), not exhaustive timetables.
- A “baseFare” intended as a plausible starting fare for a standard adult seat (or a typical walk-up passenger fare for small ferries).
- A `notes` field explicitly telling the app to prompt users to confirm on the operator booking page/counter for the travel date.

This is most important for island ferries and for minivan operations (which can reorganize by queue and day). citeturn8search17turn32search0turn35view0
